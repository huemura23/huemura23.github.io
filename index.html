Tonga Tsunami

On 20 December 2021, a submarine volcano in the Tongan archipelago in the southern Pacific Ocean named Hunga Tongaâ€“Hunga Ha'apai began its eruption. The unprecedented event, now considered the world's largest underwater volcanic eruption in three decades, severed the single cable that connects Tonga to the outside world, effectively removing all internet and phone communication for the nation's 105,000 people. As a result, information on the true extent of the damage and how the world could best help was significantly delayed. The Global Facility for Disaster Reduction and Recovery reported that 600 buildings including 300 homes were damaged or destroyed by the tsunami. The damage was estimated at US $43.7 million.
