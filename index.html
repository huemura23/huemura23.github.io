The Tonga Tsunami Disaster

After staying relatively inactive since 2014, the Hunga Tonga–Hunga Haʻapai volcano erupted on 20 December 2021, sending ash and towering tsunami waves toward the small South Pacific Tonga's Fonuafo'ou island. Satellites visually captured shockwaves propagating across the Pacific Ocean. The event, now considered the world's largest underwater volcanic eruption in three decades, severed the single cable that connects Tonga to the outside world, effectively removing all internet and phone communication for the nation's 105,000 people. As a result, information on the true extent of the damage and how the world could best help was significantly delayed. The event was probably the largest volcanic eruption in the 21st century, and the largest recorded since the 1991 eruption of Mount Pinatubo. The Global Facility for Disaster Reduction and Recovery reported that 600 buildings including 300 homes were damaged or destroyed by the tsunami. The damage was estimated at US $43.7 million.

The Impact has been catastrophic.
